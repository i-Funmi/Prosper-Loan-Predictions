Part II - (Prosper Loan Data Exploration)¶

by (Ishola Olufunmi Abiodun)¶

Investigation Overview¶

In this investigation, I would be investigating the variables or characteristics that could be used to determine the outcome of a loan, that is, if the loan would be completed, chargedoff, defaulted or pass due dates

Dataset Overview¶

The loan dataset consists of 113937 loans(rows) and 81 variables regarding each loan. Thirtyfour rows had been removed from the dataset due to incompleteness and inconsistencies. Also, columns not need for analyses had been excluded. Overall, the presentation would be based on 113903 rows and 19 columns.

Distribution of the Loan Statuses¶

Most loans were completely paid off, while about 21% of the loans were charged off. The frequency of the past due loans less than 3 months was about five times more than those above 3 months past due with cancelled loans having the lowest frequency(0.004%).

Distribution of Loan Amount¶

The distribtion of loan amount seemed of varying degree with loans starting from as low as $1k to as high as $35k. The variable when viewed with smaller bin sizes followed a unimodal distribution with most of the loans at $4k, followed by $15k, then $10k, $5k and below.

Loan Amount against Loan Status¶

It could be seen that loans above $5k were past due loans while loans below $5k were completed, defaulted or chargedoff; with cancelled loans having the lowest amount of loan. This would mean that loan as a single variable might not have much influence on loan statuses.

Loan Amount against Loan Status¶

Judging by the pattern followed by the distribution, it could be said that the annual percentage rate seemed strongly correlated with loan status and loans with an average of 0.2 APR could end up completely paid, and above 0.2 might end up cancelled, defaulted, chargedoff or passing due date.

Loan Status Vs. Loan Amount + Amount Invested by Friends¶

Even though there were many loans with zero amount investment from friends, it could be deduced that loans with higher amount of investments made by friends had a higher chance of been completed than loans with lower amount of investments from friends. The past due and cancelled loans across all loan amounts had zero amount invested by friends. There were little increases in amount invested by friends for defaulted loans, but the highest amount invested by friends was about $10k, while it was about $15k maximum for chargedoff loan, and completed loans had as high as $25k amount invested by friends.

Loan Status Vs. Loan Amount + Income Range of Borrowers¶

Borrowers who took loans below $10k and earned above $100k have a really high possibility of completing their loans, those who took about $11k might have their loans chargedoff, and if about $12k, might pass their payments due dates while those who took above $12k could get their loans defaulted. Likewise, borrowers who took loans below $8k and earned between $75k-99k have a high possibility of completing their loans, and if they took above $8k, they might end up not completing their loans. Similarly, low or no income earners who took an average of $5k loans might complete their loans compared to those who borrowed higher than that amount at that income range.
